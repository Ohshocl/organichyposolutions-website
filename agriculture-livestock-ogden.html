Northern Utah Agriculture Faces Concentrated Disease and Environmental Risk

Weber and Box Elder counties are home to some of Utah's most productive agricultural land — and some of its highest-density poultry and dairy operations. The concentration of livestock in the northern corridor creates biosecurity conditions where a single pathogen introduction can move quickly between operations. Conventional disinfectants that require animal removal and extended ventilation add operational friction that HOCl eliminates entirely.

Northern Utah's climate — cold winters with confined livestock, spring moisture events, and irrigation-intensive summer operations — creates ideal conditions for barn mold, feed mycotoxins, and pathogen buildup in livestock facilities. HOCl's ability to treat occupied facilities without withdrawal periods means Ogden-area operations can maintain consistent biosecurity protocols without disrupting production schedules.
