Frequently Asked Questions
Which disinfectant is most effective against COVID-19 and other viruses?
Hypochlorous acid (HOCl) has demonstrated superior efficacy against enveloped viruses like COVID-19, working in 15-30 seconds compared to several minutes for other disinfectants. EPA testing shows it's effective against a broader spectrum of pathogens than most alternatives, while being significantly safer for users and the environment.
Why do some disinfectants require longer contact times?
Contact time reflects how long a disinfectant needs to remain on a surface to kill pathogens effectively. Bleach requires 5-10 minutes because it works through a slower chemical process. HOCl works faster because it's the same molecule your immune system uses - optimized by evolution for rapid pathogen elimination.
What are the health concerns with traditional disinfectants?
Many conventional disinfectants contain quaternary ammonium compounds, chlorine compounds, or other chemicals that can cause respiratory irritation, skin sensitization, and other health issues with repeated exposure. Some studies link these chemicals to asthma, reproductive issues, and antimicrobial resistance when overused.
How do I choose the right disinfectant for my business?
Consider your primary concerns: staff safety, customer comfort, environmental impact, effectiveness, and regulatory compliance. Food service operations need food-safe options, healthcare requires broad-spectrum efficacy, and public spaces benefit from odorless solutions. The ideal choice balances maximum protection with minimum risk.
What about cost considerations between different disinfectants?
While some traditional disinfectants appear less expensive per gallon, total cost includes application time, protective equipment, ventilation requirements, potential health impacts, and productivity losses. Faster-acting, safer solutions often prove more economical when all factors are considered.
Are there regulatory differences between disinfectants?
Yes, EPA registration varies by product and intended use. Some disinfectants have restrictions in food preparation areas, around medical devices, or in specific industries. FDA approval for wound care (like HOCl has) indicates an even higher safety standard than basic EPA registration for surface disinfection.
