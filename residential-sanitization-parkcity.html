Why Not Just Use Store-Bought Disinfectants?

Most household disinfectants are effective on the surfaces you spray. The problem is what they leave behind — and what they miss entirely.

What conventional products leave behind

Quaternary ammonium compounds leave a chemical film on surfaces that children and pets contact. Bleach produces fumes concerning for asthma and chemical sensitivity. Neither reaches inside HVAC systems, upholstery fibers, or the airborne pathogen load that fogging addresses.

What spray-and-wipe misses

Surface wiping treats the areas you can see and reach. Professional fogging distributes HOCl as a fine mist that settles on every surface — including under furniture, inside HVAC ducts, on ceiling tiles, and into fabric fibers where pathogens and allergens actually live.

What HOCl does differently

HOCl is pH neutral — gentler than tap water at use concentration. It leaves no chemical residue, no odor, and no film. It biodegrades to simple salt water. Your family and pets can walk back in immediately after treatment. No waiting, no ventilating, no worrying about what's on the surfaces your toddler just touched.
