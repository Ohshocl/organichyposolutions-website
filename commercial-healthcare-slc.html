The Real Cost of Inadequate Commercial Disinfection

Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) cost the U.S. healthcare system billions annually. For commercial facilities, a single outbreak — flu, norovirus, MRSA — can shut down operations, damage reputation, and expose ownership to liability.

  • !
    Conventional disinfectants leave chemical residues and odors. QACs, bleach-based products, and phenolic compounds require ventilation periods, produce strong chemical smells, and can trigger respiratory issues in sensitive patients, students, and employees.
  • !
    Surface disinfection alone misses airborne pathogen reservoirs. HVAC systems, air handlers, and ceiling surfaces harbor mold spores and respiratory viruses. Professional fogging reaches surfaces that spray-and-wipe approaches cannot.
  • !
    C. difficile spores survive standard cleaning. C. diff is notoriously resistant to many common disinfectants. Healthcare facilities, senior living centers, and medical offices need a product with documented sporicidal activity.
  • !
    Chemical sensitivity affects patients, students, and staff. Immunocompromised patients, children, pregnant staff, and allergy-sensitive employees cannot tolerate the chemical burden of conventional disinfection programs.
