Salt Lake Valley Agriculture Faces Unique Biosecurity Pressures

The Salt Lake Valley sits at the crossroads of urban growth and agricultural legacy. Operations in South Jordan, Herriman, Riverton, Bluffdale, and the west side of the valley face increasing development pressure alongside genuine disease and contamination risks — and they operate within proximity to densely populated areas where biosecurity failures have public health consequences well beyond the farm gate.

Urban-edge farms in the Salt Lake Valley deal with environmental contamination vectors that rural operations don't — higher foot traffic near operations, proximity to storm drain systems that limit runoff options, and neighbors who notice when conventional disinfectants produce strong chemical odors. HOCl eliminates the odor problem entirely while providing EPA-registered antimicrobial coverage that complies with NOP organic standards.
