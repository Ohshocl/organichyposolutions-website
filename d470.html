Quick Reference — All 4 Sections
S1 — Elements of Logistics & Transportation
S1

Strategy: Logistics strategy derives from corporate business plan. 4PL = entire supply chain. 3PL = specific parts. Outsource primary reason = reduce costs.

Transport: Air = high-value fragile long-distance. Road selection = weight & volume. Marine fuel surcharge = Bunker Adjustment. Lower volume-to-weight ratio = more efficient.

PLC: Growth = greatest positive slope. Introductory = increases at increasing rate. Variability-based = demand fluctuation. Continuous production = non-stop identical product. Quality performance = minimizes waste.

Logistics types: Reverse logistics = recycling/returns. Break-bulk = large in, small out. Echelon = production→warehouse→local DC→customer. EV road tax exemption = Incentive. Minimize empty miles = pick up from suppliers AFTER drop-off.

S2 — Laws, Regulations & Cross-Border
S2

Customs: Production location = customs tax liability. Freight forwarder = prepares customs declarations. TFA = harmonizes global customs. Kyoto Convention = more predictable customs. Commodity code (HS code) = categorizes tariff rates.

Law & compliance: FCPA = corporations AND individuals, inside AND outside country. Cross-border tariffs = cost unique to international trade. BEST = improves safety in trade/supply chain. Quick Response = agile demand strategy. ECR benefit = improved service level.

S3 — TCO, Inventory & Operations (Your Weakest Area)
S3

Inventory: Cycle stock = regular expected demand for a period. Safety stock = buffer above cycle. In-process = partially finished, near production. Pipeline = in transit. Pareto fast movers at 90% availability = A line. More SKUs = product proliferation. Shortage cost = penalty for inadequate stock.

Measurement: Moving average = average of past N periods actual demand. Operating control system = KPI vs standard + corrective action (NOT SCOR). Validity = data missing stages. Hierarchy of needs = serves different management LEVELS. Benchmarking = vs world-class standards. QFD = customer requirements → supplier performance. Malcolm Baldridge = US excellence framework.

Outsourcing: Outsource to focus on core = decrease in logistics decision-making control. Outsource assets = investment opportunities. Multiuser 3PL = economies of scale. Unknown 3PL + strong brand = brand interference risk. First step = identify service requirements and costs. Monitor via audits = performance tracking. % of profit for exceeding targets = gainsharing.

Other S3: R&D on new products = new product introduction (Learning & Innovation). Donor = provides funds. Agency = delivers. Notified when distributed (not onsite).

S4 — Warehouse Management
S4

Systems: WMS = all in-building ops (picking, put-away, RF, inventory). TMS = transport routing. YMS = yard/dock/vehicle. OMS = customer orders.

Operations: Cantilevered racking = long items, low cost. Picking = most labor-intensive. Individual picker gap = picking method. Facility gap = picking equipment usage. Geofencing = automates bay assignment. Short-term planning = workload based on order receipts. Safety metric = days without an accident. Put-away time = receipt to storage ONLY. Higher turnover ratio = lower inventory.
