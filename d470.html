Variability-based vs Volume-based strategy (Q9)
MISSED Q9S1

You picked Volume-based. Correct answer = Variability-based.

Large demand fluctuation is the trigger word — it always points to Variability-based strategy, designed specifically to flex output up and down with unpredictable demand. Volume-based is for steady, high, predictable demand (economies of scale).

Break-bulk vs Postponement (Q16)
MISSED Q16S1

You picked Postponement. Correct answer = Break-bulk.

Break-bulk = large shipments in, smaller shipments out (consolidation → redistribution). Postponement = delaying final customization/configuration until closer to the order — it's about WHEN work happens, not about splitting shipment size.

Trigger phrase "receives large shipments... ships out smaller shipments" = Break-bulk, every time.
Quick Response (agile demand strategy) (Q28)
MISSED Q28S2

You picked 5S concept. Correct answer = Quick Response.

5S is a workplace organization methodology (Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain) — not a demand-response strategy. Quick Response is the agile strategy that shortens replenishment cycles to react to actual demand changes.

BEST (Border Enforcement Security Task Force) (Q31)
MISSED Q31S2

You picked World customs organizations. Correct answer = BEST.

World Customs Organization (WCO) sets global standards (like the Kyoto Convention) but isn't specifically a safety/security enforcement task force. BEST is the named US inter-agency group whose specific mission is improving SAFETY in international trade and supply chain.

In-process vs Pipeline stock (Q33)
MISSED Q33S3

You picked Pipeline. Correct answer = In-process.

Pipeline stock = goods currently moving/in transit between locations. In-process stock = partially finished goods held NEAR production so they can be rapidly completed once ordered. The 1-hour-delivery, partially-finished detail = In-process, not Pipeline.

Key distinction: Pipeline = "on the way." In-process = "almost done, sitting close by."
Cycle Stock vs Safety Stock (Q34)
MISSED Q34S3

You picked Safety. Correct answer = Cycle.

Cycle stock = stock equivalent to the typical/regular expected demand for a period (a quarter, a month). Safety stock = the extra BUFFER held above that regular demand to cover uncertainty. "Stock equivalent to typical demand for one quarter" describes the baseline expected need = Cycle, not the buffer.

Product proliferation vs order cycle time (Q39)

More product VARIETY = more SKUs = more product proliferation = each SKU needs its own inventory = rising total inventory cost. Order cycle time is about how long fulfillment takes, unrelated to variety growth.

WMS vs YMS for put-away efficiency (Q70)
MISSED Q70S4

You picked Yard management system (YMS). Correct answer = Warehouse management system (WMS).

YMS manages vehicles/dock bays in the YARD — outside the building. Put-away (placing received stock into storage locations) is a WAREHOUSE floor activity, controlled by the WMS. Don't confuse yard logistics with in-building storage logistics.

Anything about put-away, picking, RF communications inside the four walls = WMS. Anything about dock bays, vehicle check-in, gate management = YMS.
Section 1 — Elements of Distribution, Logistics & Transportation
Logistics vs Distribution vs Supply Chain — scope hierarchy
S1 Core

Logistics = planning and managing the flow of goods, services, and information from origin to consumption. Distribution = the subset of logistics focused specifically on getting finished goods from manufacturer/warehouse out to customers. Supply Chain = the widest scope — includes all parties from raw material suppliers through end customers.

Logistics strategy is derived from the corporate business plan — it doesn't exist independently.
Logistics providers — 1PL, 2PL, 3PL, 4PL
S1 Core
3PL
Specific parts of supply chain: transportation, warehousing.
4PL
ENTIRE supply chain. Orchestrates 3PLs.

Why outsource? Primary reason = to reduce logistics costs.

Transportation modes & volume-to-weight ratio
S1 Core

Air = high-value, fragile, time-sensitive, long distance. Road selection factors: weight and volume. Bunker Adjustment Surcharge = marine fuel variation.

Lower volume-to-weight ratio = denser product = MORE efficient distribution.

Product Life Cycle (PLC)
S1 Core

Growth = greatest positive slope. Introductory = sales increase at an increasing rate. Maturity = increasing at a decreasing rate. Decline = decreasing.

Manufacturing & performance strategies
S1 Core

Continuous, non-stop, identical-product manufacturing (chemicals) = Continuous production. Quality performance minimizes waste. Reengineering to lower costs = fewer facilities, longer lead times.

Logistics types & distribution operations
S1 Core

Reverse logistics = returns/recycling. Break-bulk = large in, small out. Cross-docking = no storage. Echelon = production→warehouse→local DC→customer multi-tier flow. Incentive = government reward (EV tax exemption). Minimize empty miles = pick up from suppliers AFTER drop-off to customers.

Section 2 — Laws, Regulations & Cross-Border Operations
Customs, tariffs & trade agreements
S2 Core

Production location drives customs tax liability (country of origin). Freight forwarder prepares customs declarations. TFA harmonizes global customs procedures. Kyoto Convention goal = make customs more predictable. Commodity code (HS code) categorizes tariff rates. Improving declarations/documentation speeds clearance.

FCPA (1977): corporations AND individuals, inside AND outside the country.

Cross-border tariffs cost = unique cost of international trade. BEST = safety/security agency. Quick Response = agile demand strategy. ECR benefit = improved service level. Poor border control → increased product loss in market (counterfeits).
