Allergen Reduction & Atopic Dermatitis Research

Study: "Hypochlorous acid is antipruritic and anti‐inflammatory in a mouse model of atopic dermatitis"

Published: Clinical & Experimental Allergy, Wiley Online Library, 2018

Key Findings: HOCl hydrogel treatment prevented development of allergen-induced lesions and significantly reduced scratching behavior in house dust mite sensitized mice. Topical HOCl showed anti-inflammatory properties and reduced IgE levels associated with allergic reactions.*

Allergen-Induced Lesion Prevention Reduced IgE Levels Anti-Inflammatory Action
Dust Mite Allergen Denaturation

Study: "Allergenic proteins are fragmented in low concentrations of sodium hypochlorite"

Published: Journal of Allergy Research, 2001

Key Findings: Hypochlorite solutions effectively destroy dust mites and denature protein allergens. Children living in homes regularly cleaned with hypochlorite-based products showed reduced rates of asthma (OR, 0.10), eczema (OR, 0.22), and sensitization to indoor allergens, especially house dust mites (OR, 0.43).*

Protein Allergen Denaturation Dust Mite Destruction Reduced Asthma Risk
Airborne Allergen Neutralization

Published: Environmental Health Research, 2025

Key Findings: HOCl fogging effectively neutralizes airborne allergens including dust mites, pollen, and mold spores. Regular application creates more comfortable environments for allergy sufferers by reducing triggers for allergic reactions.*

Airborne Allergen Reduction Pollen Neutralization Mold Spore Elimination
Safety & Toxicology Research

Key Findings: No adverse effects observed in acute, subchronic, or chronic exposure studies. Non-irritating to skin and eyes. Safe for inhalation at working concentrations. Biodegrades to harmless salt water.*

Non-Toxic to Humans Non-Irritating Biodegradable
