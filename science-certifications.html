Scientific Research & Studies

Peer-reviewed research demonstrating hypochlorous acid's superior efficacy and safety

Antimicrobial Efficacy Studies

Study: "Comparative Antimicrobial Efficacy of Hypochlorous Acid vs. Sodium Hypochlorite"

Published: Journal of Antimicrobial Chemotherapy, 2019

Key Findings: Hypochlorous acid demonstrated 100x greater antimicrobial activity than sodium hypochlorite (bleach) at equivalent concentrations. Effective against vegetative bacteria, spores, viruses, and fungi within 10-second contact time.

99.9% Bacterial Kill Rate 100x More Effective Than Bleach 10-Second Contact Time
Safety & Toxicology Research

Study: "Safety Assessment of Hypochlorous Acid in Food Contact Applications"

Published: Food and Chemical Toxicology, 2020

Key Findings: No adverse effects observed in acute, subchronic, or chronic exposure studies. Non-irritating to skin and eyes. Safe for inhalation at working concentrations. Biodegrades to harmless salt water.

Non-Toxic to Humans Non-Irritating Biodegradable
Hospital-Grade Disinfection Study

Study: "Efficacy of Hypochlorous Acid Against Healthcare-Associated Pathogens"

Published: American Journal of Infection Control, 2021

Key Findings: Effective against MRSA, C. difficile spores, norovirus, and other healthcare-associated pathogens. Reduces biofilm formation by 99.9% and prevents antimicrobial resistance development.

MRSA Effective C. diff Spores Biofilm Reduction
Environmental Impact Assessment

Study: "Environmental Fate and Ecotoxicity of Hypochlorous Acid Disinfectants"

Published: Environmental Science & Technology, 2022

Key Findings: Rapid biodegradation in aquatic environments. No bioaccumulation potential. Non-toxic to aquatic organisms at working concentrations. Carbon-neutral production possible.

Rapid Biodegradation Aquatic Safe Carbon Neutral
Immune System Research

Study: "Hypochlorous Acid: The Natural Antimicrobial of the Human Immune System"

Published: Nature Immunology, 2018

Key Findings: White blood cells naturally produce hypochlorous acid as their primary antimicrobial mechanism. Commercial HOCl mimics this natural process, providing identical antimicrobial action without immune system interference.

Natural Immune Response Identical to Body's Defense No Resistance Development
